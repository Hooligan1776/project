Skiing

The adventure of a lifetime

There are skiers of all different types and varities. Of all skiers, however, three main skill distinctions exist:

  1. Beginner
  2. Intermediate
  3. Expert

When picking the right type of skis and ski settings, it is important to be honest about your skill level. Your skill level directly affects your DIN variable, which dictates how much force it takes for your boot to pop out of your ski when you fall or catch an edge on your ski. Too high of a number for too low of a skill level can lead to broken bones, and in extreme cases, death.


Once you've figured out your correct skill level, and adjusted your settings accordingly, it is important to recognize the different types of terrain you can come across, and the things you can do with that terrain.

  1. Groomers - Straight and gently sloped runs allowing you to pick up lots of speed and practice your turns
  2. Moguls - High-octane ruts which test your ability to make technical turns with short time available.
  3. Tricks - Various jumps located across the mountain allow you to attempt some sick tricks. Some tricks can include:
    • Frontflips
    • Backflips
    • 360° Spins
